China's Open-Source AI Contributions vs the United States

A response to "China only cares about China, and if they are the world leaders in AI, the rest of the world is fucked."

China is currently the world's leading contributor to open-source AI, releasing frontier models under the most permissive licenses available (MIT, Apache 2.0), while the US keeps its best models locked behind proprietary APIs. The data speaks for itself.

By the Numbers

9 of 14 Top global AI models are Chinese and open-source. The US has zero open-source models in the top 14.

Source: Forbes, April 2026
2.7% The US-China AI performance gap collapsed from 30+ percentage points in 2023 to just 2.7% as of March 2026.

Source: Stanford HAI AI Index 2026
71.5% Of the top 1,000 models on Hugging Face ship under a permissive open license. Apache 2.0 alone covers 49%; MIT covers 19.8%. Qwen's Apache 2.0 strategy attracts the global developer audience that Meta's restrictive "Community License" repels.

China's top AI labs released their frontier models under MIT and Apache 2.0 licenses, giving the world free, modifiable, commercially usable AI that anyone can download, run, study, and build on. The US's top frontier models (GPT-5, Claude, Gemini) are entirely proprietary. You pay for API access and can never inspect, modify, or redistribute them. Meta's Llama uses a custom license with a 700 million user cap that disqualifies it as true open source.

China gave the world free, modifiable frontier AI. The US sold API keys.
